OK. *Monday afternoon at Mar-a-Lago.* The ballroom. The press pool in the back. The Secretary of War, the Secretary of the Navy, and the Secretary of State, in suits, *to the President's left.* The President, at the lectern, *announces a new class of Navy battleship.*

*The Trump-class battleship.* That is the name. *Trump-class.* Named after the *sitting* President. The lead ship will be the *USS Defiant.* The Navy press release, on Monday, confirms the class name, the lead ship name, and the schedule. *The Navy press release is the Navy press release.* I am not making this up. *I cannot make this up.*

*Buddy.* The U.S. Navy has *not built a battleship* since the *Iowa-class,* which was *commissioned in the 1940s.* The Iowa-class was the *Mighty Mo* and her sisters. The last one, *Wisconsin,* was decommissioned in 1991. The Navy has, for thirty-four years, *not had battleships,* on the doctrine that the *aircraft carrier* and the *guided-missile destroyer* are the *modern surface combatants* and the *battleship* is, in the language of the Navy War College, *of historical interest.* The Navy War College stands by that.

The *Trump-class,* the President said, will be *the fastest, the biggest, and 100 times more powerful than any battleship ever built.* On the record. *One hundred times more powerful* is, in the technical sense, *not a unit of measurement.* The *Iowa-class* main batteries fired *2,700-pound shells.* The *Trump-class,* per the press release, will fire *guided missiles* and, in the future, *electromagnetic railguns,* on the assumption that railguns *exist by then,* which the Office of Naval Research, on the public record, *does not currently assume.*

*The schedule.* First-of-class construction *will not begin until 2030.* That is *five years from now.* That is *the next presidential term, plus a year.* The first ship will not be *afloat* until, by the Navy's projection, the *late 2030s.* The full class of *twenty to twenty-five ships* will not be in service until *the 2050s.* The *2050s.*

*The President,* asked at the lectern *what role he would play in the design of the new ship,* said, *I am a very aesthetic person, I would design the ship along with the Navy.* *Buddy.* Aesthetic. *That is the word he used.* He is going to *help design the battleship* because he is *aesthetic.*

I have run a casino floor for thirty years. I have seen *plenty* of high rollers think they know how to *design the slot machine* on the basis of the *trim around the screen.* The trim around the screen is *not* the slot machine. The Navy, *per the Navy War College,* does not need a *new battleship.* What the Navy needs is *more submarines.* The submarines, in the press release, *got a paragraph.* The Trump-class got *the wholepage.*

I am arguing with the television. The television is in *Palm Beach.* The Palm Beach is *aesthetic.*

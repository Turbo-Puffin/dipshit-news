A man came into the diner once with a laminated card showing his bowling score from 1994. He kept it in his wallet, behind the pictures of his kids. He would show you the kids if you asked. He would show you the bowling score whether you asked or not.

I thought about that man this weekend.

## The test.

The President had his annual physical at Walter Reed. Part of it is a cognitive test, the kind a doctor gives to check for early signs of trouble. You name some animals. You draw a clock. You hear five words and try to say them back a few minutes later. A perfect score is thirty, and it means the doctor found nothing to worry about. It does not mean you are a genius. It means you are fine.

The President scored a thirty. Good for him. Genuinely.

## The math.

Then he went on Truth Social, late, and did some arithmetic. He has taken the test four times. Four times thirty is one hundred and twenty. So he announced, and I am quoting him, "120 correct answers out of 120 questions asked," and he called the result "extreme intelligence."

Sit with that for a second. He multiplied one score by the number of times he sat the exam and reported the total like a season stat. By that logic I have parallel parked nine thousand times without hitting the curb, and I would like my trophy now.

## What the test actually is.

Here is the part the doctors keep saying into the wind. The test is named for the city in Canada where they wrote it, and it screens for dementia. A man on CNN who gives the thing for a living said a score of twenty-six or higher just means normal. Normal. Not gifted, not extreme, not a card you keep behind the kids.

The President's own doctor wrote that he is in excellent health and fit for the job. That was the news. That was the win. A clean bill of health, handed to a man who read it and decided it said something it does not say.

## The wallet.

I do not doubt the thirty. I doubt the framing, and the framing is the whole thing. There is a difference between passing the test and topping the curve, and that difference is the entire story of a certain kind of man. He gets the good news. He cannot leave it good. He has to laminate it.

Hon. Saturday night. The President of the United States, on his social media platform, posted that he is sending *a great hospital boat* to *Greenland.* The post included a picture. The picture, hon, was *AI-generated.* The picture showed *the USNS Mercy.*

The USNS Mercy, in *real life,* on Saturday night, on the *Saturday night this post went up,* was *dry-docked* in *Mobile, Alabama.* So was the *USNS Comfort,* the only other Navy hospital ship the United States has. *Both ships.* In *Alabama.* In a *shipyard.* On *publicly available shipping data.* You can *look it up.* *Anybody can look it up.*

The President said the ship would *take care of the many people who are sick* in Greenland and *not being taken care of there.* This is the *premise* of the *post.* The premise is that the *people of Greenland* are *not being taken care of.*

Now. Hon. *Pay attention.* Greenland, an *autonomous Danish territory,* operates under the *Danish national healthcare system,* which is, on every published metric, *free at the point of service for every citizen.* The *Greenlandic Prime Minister,* a gentleman named *Jens-Frederik Nielsen,* responded *the next morning,* publicly, *on the record,* with the following sentence: *we have a public healthcare system where treatment is free for citizens. It is a deliberate choice and a fundamental part of our society.*

Hon. The *Prime Minister of Greenland,* who *exists,* and who has a *job,* spent his *Sunday morning* explaining to the *President of the United States,* who *also exists* and who *also has a job,* that the citizens of his country *already get free healthcare.*

So. To recap. The *post* claimed a *thing* about *Greenlandic healthcare* that was *the opposite of true.* The *post* offered to *send* a *ship* that was *not in the water.* The *post* used a *picture* that was *AI-generated.* The *Prime Minister of Greenland* corrected the *thing.* The *USNS Mercy* did not *go anywhere.* The *next* day, the President referred to the situation again, on a Tuesday, and *the ship was still in Alabama.*

This is the *kind* of thing where, in *2009,* the *next morning's* *Today Show* would have *opened* with it. *Matt Lauer* would have *raised an eyebrow.* The Prime Minister of Greenland would have been *interviewed* live. There would have been a *graphic* of the dry-docked ship.

Now? The *Saturday night post* is a *Sunday morning hangover.* The cable hour gives it *forty seconds.* Monday it is *over.*

You ever notice how the Greenland we keep being told is *desperate* keeps *politely declining* the *gift baskets?*

Funny how that works.

Saturday morning, the President of the United States went on his social network and posted a movie poster. The movie was *Apocalypse Now,* the 1979 Francis Ford Coppola film about the American military experience in Vietnam. The poster had been Photoshopped. The President's own face was on it. He was wearing a military uniform he has not worn. The skyline behind him was on fire. The skyline was Chicago. The caption was *Chipocalypse Now.* Underneath, in his own words: *Chicago is about to find out why it's called the Department of War.*

Hon. Look. *Apocalypse Now* is not a triumph movie. It is a movie about Marlon Brando in the jungle losing his mind. The whole point of the movie, when it came out, was that the American mission was unwinnable, the war was a mistake, and the Army was, by the end of it, hauling its own colonel out of the dark in a body bag.

You do not put your face on the *Apocalypse Now* poster as a *flex.* That is the *cautionary* poster. That is the poster on the wall in the lieutenant's office that reminds him not to march into the swamp.

The other matter. Chicago, like DC, has *the lowest violent crime rate in thirty years.* Not the highest. The lowest. The Justice Department's own data. The reason this is a meme on a Saturday morning instead of a policy paper is because the policy paper would have to start with *crime is down, here is why we are going in anyway,* and that paper would not write itself.

The mayor of Chicago responded by sundown. He used the word *authoritarianism.* The President posted again that night. He did not take the meme down.

You ever notice how the people who say they want law and order keep posting the *war* movie.

Funny how that works.

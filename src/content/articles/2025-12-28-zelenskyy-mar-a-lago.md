OK. *Sunday at Mar-a-Lago.* The President of Ukraine, the President of the United States, and a small delegation. *Three hours of meetings.* Before the meetings, the President of the United States *talked to the President of Russia for over an hour by phone.* That is *the order in which it happened.* Russia first, then Ukraine.

After the three hours, the President of the United States, at the press availability, said the Russia-Ukraine peace deal is *closer than ever.* He said *ninety-five percent of the issues have been settled.* He acknowledged there is a *thorny* remaining five percent. He said the *thorny* part is *territory.*

*Buddy.* Let me translate. The *territory* part means *which country the towns of Donbas, Luhansk, Donetsk, Crimea, Zaporizhzhia, and Kherson belong to.* That is the question. That is *not five percent.* That is, *on the map,* about *one hundred and twenty thousand square kilometers* of land. Approximately the size of *North Carolina.* The question of *which country* North Carolina belongs to is *not five percent of any peace deal.* That *is* the peace deal. The other ninety-five percent is *the cover sheet.*

The cover sheet, by the way, is *fine.* The cover sheet *includes* prisoner-of-war exchanges, which Ukraine wants. *Includes* a security framework around Ukraine, which Ukraine wants. *Includes* economic reconstruction commitments, which Ukraine wants. *All cover sheet.* The territory part, the *one* part, is the part *Russia* wants. *Russia wants the towns.* Russia *currently has* about *eighty percent* of those towns under occupation. Russia is *not* going to leave them.

The President's framing, *closer than ever,* is, in the *negotiating sense,* *the deal is the same deal it was in March.* In March, when the *first* set of conversations happened, the *territory* question was the *thorny* part. In *June,* when the *second* set of conversations happened, the *territory* question was the *thorny* part. In *October,* when the *Budapest summit was scheduled and then unscheduled,* the *territory* question was the *thorny* part. In *December,* on Sunday, *the same.*

I have run a casino floor for *thirty years.* I have negotiated with vendors *every Tuesday* for *thirty years.* When the vendor and I have agreed on *everything except the price,* we have *not agreed on the deal.* We have agreed *on the cover sheet.* The price is the deal. The price has not moved.

The President, after the press availability, returned to the golf course. The President of Ukraine returned to *Florida airport, then to Warsaw, then to Kyiv, then to a country that is, this morning, still at war.* The press, after the press availability, returned to the press van. The press van returned to the press hotel. The press hotel returned the press to the same story they have been writing since *March.*

I am arguing with the television. The television is, on Sunday night, *closer than ever.*

Pay attention. This one is real. *Helicopters.* Drones in the air. Federal agents on the ground. Rifles. Shackles. The location: a Hyundai-LG Energy battery plant in Bryan County, Georgia, *under construction.*

The number arrested: *four hundred and seventy-five.* The number that turned out to be *South Korean nationals on legal visas:* north of three hundred. The country whose nationals were just shackled at an *American* plant being built by *one of our biggest manufacturing investors:* South Korea. *South Korea.*

Buddy. *Buddy.* The South Korean foreign ministry filed a formal protest by sundown. Their president called it *unacceptable.* They sent a chartered Korean Air jet to fly the workers home. Three hundred plus people, in seats, going back across the Pacific, *because we shackled them.* The reporting from the workers afterward was that ICE agents were making *rocket man* jokes during the arrests. *Rocket man jokes.* Out loud. With the cuffs on.

I have run a casino floor. I will tell you something. When you pull a bust on the floor, you do it *quietly,* you do it *correctly,* and you *make damn sure* you didn't just bust the *whole table of high rollers* by mistake. You especially make sure when the high rollers are *guests of the house* who *just bought into the room.*

This was the high rollers. South Korea is the country we just announced a *350 billion dollar* investment package with. South Korea is the country building the *EV plant* in Georgia that the same President took *credit for at a ribbon cutting.* South Korea is the country we *want more factories from.* The same week we shackled three hundred of their workers in front of camera drones.

Two weeks later the same President pivoted and said *of course* foreign experts should be welcome. The plane had landed in Seoul by then. The shackles came off in Atlanta.

I'm arguing with the television. The television is showing the helicopter footage on a loop.
